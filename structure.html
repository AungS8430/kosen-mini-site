Program Overview

The KOSEN-KMITL program is a unique 5-year integrated educational system that combines high school and undergraduate engineering education. This Japanese-style "Kosen" (College of Technology) model focuses on practical, hands-on engineering skills from an early age.

Students enter the program after completing junior high school (Grade 9) and graduate with an Associate Degree in Engineering after 5 years of intensive study. The curriculum emphasizes:

  • Practical Skills: Over 60% of class time is devoted to laboratory and workshop activities
  • Early Specialization: Students choose their engineering field from Year 1
  • Japanese Methodology: Curriculum designed in collaboration with Japanese KOSEN institutions
  • Bilingual Education: Courses taught in both English and Japanese

Important: Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.50 to remain in good academic standing throughout the program.

Our Three Departments

KOSEN-KMITL offers three specialized engineering departments. Each department provides a comprehensive 5-year curriculum designed to produce industry-ready engineers with practical skills and theoretical knowledge.

Mechatronics Engineering

Combines mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and control engineering. Graduates are prepared to design and develop smart machines, robots, and automated systems for Industry 4.0.

Key Areas: Robotics, Automation, Control Systems, IoT, Smart Manufacturing, PLC Programming, CAD/CAM

Career Paths: Robotics Engineer, Automation Engineer, Control Systems Engineer, Manufacturing Engineer, Industrial IoT Specialist

Computer Engineering

Focuses on both hardware and software aspects of computing. Students learn to design computer systems, develop software applications, and work with emerging technologies like AI and cloud computing.

Key Areas: Software Development, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Computer Networks, Cybersecurity, Embedded Systems, Cloud Computing

Career Paths: Software Developer, Systems Architect, Network Engineer, AI/ML Engineer, Embedded Systems Developer, DevOps Engineer

Electrical & Electronics Engineering

Covers the fundamentals of electrical systems, power electronics, telecommunications, and signal processing. Students gain expertise in both power systems and communication technologies.

Key Areas: Power Systems, Electronics Design, Telecommunications, Signal Processing, Renewable Energy, Power Electronics, Circuit Design

Career Paths: Power Systems Engineer, Electronics Design Engineer, Telecommunications Engineer, Signal Processing Engineer, Renewable Energy Specialist
