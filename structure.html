Program Overview

The KOSEN-KMITL program is a unique 5-year integrated educational system that combines high school and undergraduate engineering education. This Japanese-style "Kosen" (College of Technology) model focuses on practical, hands-on engineering skills from an early age.

Students enter the program after completing junior high school (Grade 9) and graduate with an Associate Degree in Engineering after 5 years of intensive study. The curriculum emphasizes:

  • Practical Skills: Over 60% of class time is devoted to laboratory and workshop activities
  • Early Specialization: Students choose their engineering field from Year 1
  • Japanese Methodology: Curriculum designed in collaboration with Japanese KOSEN institutions
  • Bilingual Education: Courses taught in both English and Japanese

Important: Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.50 to remain in good academic standing throughout the program.

Departments & Specializations

Mechatronics Engineering

Combines mechanical engineering, electronics, computer science, and control engineering. Graduates are prepared to design and develop smart machines, robots, and automated systems.

Career Paths: Robotics Engineer, Automation Engineer, Control Systems Engineer, Manufacturing Engineer

Computer Engineering

Focuses on both hardware and software aspects of computing. Students learn to design computer systems, develop software applications, and work with emerging technologies.

Career Paths: Software Developer, Systems Architect, Network Engineer, AI/ML Engineer, Embedded Systems Developer

Electrical & Electronics Engineering

Covers the fundamentals of electrical systems, power electronics, telecommunications, and signal processing. Students gain expertise in both power and communication systems.

Career Paths: Power Systems Engineer, Electronics Design Engineer, Telecommunications Engineer, Signal Processing Engineer

Students must complete a minimum of 170 credits with a cumulative GPA of 2.00 or higher to graduate.

Additional Graduation Requirements

  • Pass Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) N3 or higher
  • Pass English Proficiency Test (TOEIC 550 or equivalent)
  • Complete 320 hours of industrial internship
  • Successfully defend capstone project
  • No outstanding academic or disciplinary issues
